\subsection{Touch}

\subsubsection{\bh{måšaš}}

\begin{paper}
	The verb \bh{måšaš} ‘to touch (by hand)’ is translated in most cases using \C{teimlo}, as we’ve seen in the list of modalities in chapter 115 of Psalms. \C{palfalu} or \C{ymbalfalu} are used in verses concerning trying to grasp the environment in darkness or blindness. \C{gafel} \tounfold{***}
\end{paper}

\subsubsection{\bh{giššēš}}

\begin{paper}
	The verb \bh{giššēš} has only one\tounfold{לבדוק} occurance in the Bible, which is similar to \bh{måšaš} used for grasping the environment in darkness or blindness; accordingly it is translated by \C{palfalu}.
\end{paper}

\begin{example}{Isa.}{59}{10}{}{}
	\quoling
	{\lhl{נְגַֽשְׁשָׁ֤ה} כַֽעִוְרִים֙ קִ֔יר וּכְאֵ֥ין עֵינַ֖יִם \lhl{נְגַשֵּׁ֑שָׁה} כָּשַׁ֤לְנוּ בַֽצָּהֳרַ֙יִם֙ כַּנֶּ֔שֶׁף בָּאַשְׁמַנִּ֖ים כַּמֵּתִֽים׃}
	{\lhl{Palfalaſom} fell deillion a’r pared, ie fel [rhai] heb lygaid y \lhl{palfalaſom} {palfalafom}: tramgwyddaſom ar hanner dydd fel y cyfnos, [oeddem] ym myſc y beddau fel rhai meirw.}
	{\tounfold{***}}
	{\tounfold{***}}
\end{example}
